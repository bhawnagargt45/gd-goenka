NATURE










Land is constantly being cleared to meet the world's increasing food demands. With current diets, nearly 90 per cent of land animals could lose some of their habitat by 2050.






By 2050 world population is expected to increase from 7 billion today to over 9 billion . A growing population is likely to increase pressures on the natural resources that supply energy and food.





Between now and 2050, we will continue to see an increase in the environmental and climate-related hazards that are a major concern today. These hazards are innumerable but can be broken down into five broad categories: Increased drought and wildfires. Increased flooding and extreme weather.
